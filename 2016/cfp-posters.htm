Call for Posters

We invite you to participate in the 2016 IEEE Symposium on 3D User Interfaces (http://www.3dui.org) by submitting a poster. Posters provide an informal but interactive forum in which authors can present their work to attendees during the poster session and conference breaks. Posters describe works-in-progress, novel ideas, novel systems, or present the latest implemented applications. Posters are a great way to let others in the 3DUI community know about your work as well as an opportunity to discuss and make potential collaborations!

Poster submissions should be within the scope of three-dimensional user interfaces. Topics of the symposium include (but are not limited to):

  • 3D input devices
  • 3D display and sensory feedback (all five senses)
  • 3D interaction techniques
  • 3D user interface metaphors
  • Mobile 3DUIs
  • Hybrid 3DUIs
  • Non-fatiguing 3DUIs
  • Desktop 3DUIs
  • 3DUIs for VR and AR
  • Evaluation methods for 3DUIs
  • Human perception of 3D interaction
  • Collaborative 3D interaction
  • Software technologies to support 3DUIs
  • Applications of 3DUIs: Games, entertainment, CAD, education, etc.

Authors must submit a two-page abstract in the 3DUI format (http://junctionpublishing.org/vgtc/Track/3DUI.html). It is recommended that authors additionally submit a draft of the actual poster in pdf format. Poster sizes will be no more than 36” x 48” (any orientation). Accepted abstracts will appear in the proceedings. Submit materials at http://precisionconference.com/~vr

Important Dates:

  • Posters Abstract Due – Extended to January 4, 2016 (midnight PST)
  • Author Notification – January 22, 2016
  • Final Camera-Ready Abstracts - February 1, 2016 (midnight PST)

Authors of accepted poster submissions are expected to prepare a final version of their two-page abstract and present their poster at 3DUI in a special poster session and at designated coffee breaks. Authors will also give a 1-minute presentation of their poster during a poster fast forward session, which will require submission of a presentation slide prior to the symposium.
