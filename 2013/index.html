About 3DUI 2013

The IEEE Symposium on 3D User Interfaces 2013 is the eighth international symposium focused on the topic of 3D User Interfaces (3D UI). It provides an unique opportunity for industrial and academic researchers to exchange about the state-of-the-art 3DUI research!

The symposium will last for two days, and will be followed immediately by the IEEE Virtual Reality 2013 conference. All areas of 3D UI research will be presented, including:

  • 3D input devices
  • 3D display and sensory feedback
  • 3D interaction techniques
  • 3D user interface metaphors
  • Desktop 3D UIs
  • 3D UIs for VR and AR
  • Evaluation methods for 3D UIs
  • Biologically inspired 3D UIs
  • Human perception of 3D interaction
  • Collaborative 3D interaction
  • Applications of 3D UIs: games, entertainment, CAD, education, etc

Researchers can participate in a number of ways, including submitting full technical papers, technotes, and posters. In addition, there will be our annual 3D User Interface competition: the 3DUI Contest.

The authors of best papers from 3DUI 2013 will be invited to submit extended versions to the International Journal of Human-Computer Studies (IJHCS) and IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Graphics (IEEE TVCG).

Looking forward to meeting you at the 3DUI 2013 Symposium!

Anatole Lécuyer, Frank Steinicke, Mark Billinghurst
3DUI 2013 Symposium Chairs
